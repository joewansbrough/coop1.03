# Co-op SaaS Go-to-Market Strategy
## From Pilot to Preferred Vendor in 24 Months

---

## Executive Summary

**Mission**: Become the standard governance platform for BC housing cooperatives by 2028, serving 100+ co-ops (~40% market share) and expanding to other provinces.

**Strategy**: Land design partner co-ops → build case studies → leverage CHF BC partnership → dominate BC market → expand nationally.

**Timeline**: 24 months from pilot launch to market leadership position.

**Target Market**: 250+ housing co-ops in BC, ranging from 12 to 300+ units.

---

## Market Analysis

### Market Size & Segmentation

**Total Market**: ~250 BC housing co-ops

**Segmentation by Size**:
- Small (12-30 units): ~40 co-ops — lowest budget, highest pain (volunteer burnout)
- Medium (31-100 units): ~140 co-ops — sweet spot (budget + complexity)
- Large (101-200 units): ~50 co-ops — highest budget, most sophisticated needs
- Very Large (200+ units): ~20 co-ops — custom requirements, highest revenue

**Geographic Concentration**:
- Greater Vancouver: ~120 co-ops (48%)
- Greater Victoria: ~40 co-ops (16%)
- Rest of BC: ~90 co-ops (36%)

**Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)** for Pilots:
- 40-150 units
- Active, engaged board (not burnt out/apathetic)
- Recent board turnover pain (lost institutional knowledge)
- Currently using spreadsheets/Google Drive/email (not entrenched software)
- Located in Victoria or Vancouver (easier support during pilot)
- Member of CHF BC (already values sector resources)

### Competitive Landscape

**Direct Competitors**: NONE  
No BC-specific or co-op-specific governance platforms exist.

**Indirect Competitors**:
1. **Property management software** (Yardi, Buildium, AppFolio)
   - Landlord-focused, expensive, complex, poor fit
   - Opportunity: Position against their weaknesses

2. **Generic tools** (Google Drive, Excel, email)
   - Free, familiar, but chaotic and inefficient
   - Opportunity: Demonstrate ROI vs. volunteer time

3. **Custom solutions** (co-ops that built their own)
   - High maintenance, single-person dependency, not scalable
   - Opportunity: Offer turnkey alternative with support

4. **Status quo / inertia**
   - "We've always done it this way"
   - Opportunity: Change agents on boards (new, younger members)

**Barriers to Entry**:
- Low for competitors (anyone can build software)
- High for you to scale (domain knowledge is the moat)

**Your Moat**:
- Co-op governance domain expertise (Rules, CAA, CHF BC, AGM procedures)
- BC-specific compliance and resources pre-loaded
- Network effects (CHF BC endorsement unlocks sector)
- First-mover advantage and early case studies

## Phase 1: Pilot Program (Q2 2026, 3 months)

**Goal**: 3-5 pilot co-ops, validate product-market fit, build case studies

### Target Pilot Partners

**Criteria**:
- 40-150 units (complex enough to show value, small enough to onboard quickly)
- Geographic diversity (at least one Vancouver, one Victoria, one other)
- Size diversity (one small, 2-3 medium, one large)
- Active boards willing to provide monthly feedback
- Identifiable pain points we can solve

**Sourcing**:
1. **Direct network**: Oak Bay Housing Co-op (already engaged)
2. **CHF BC referrals**: Ask for intros from CHF BC staff
3. **Warm intros**: Mutual connections in co-op sector
4. **LinkedIn**: Target board chairs and administrators
5. **CHF BC events**: Spring Regional Meetings, conferences

**Outreach Tactics**:
- Personalized emails to board chairs (see templates)
- LinkedIn connection requests to administrators
- Attend CHF BC regional meetings (Vancouver, Victoria)
- Post in CHF BC member forums (with permission)
- Ask existing contacts for referrals

### Pilot Execution

**Week 1-2**: Recruitment
- Send 30 outreach emails
- Attend 2 CHF BC events
- Goal: 10 discovery calls → 5 pilot agreements

**Week 3-4**: Onboarding (per co-op)
- Data migration assistance
- Customization (upload policies, rules, minutes)
- Board training session (2 hours)
- Member rollout communications

**Week 5-12**: Active Pilot
- Monthly check-in calls (30 min)
- Support requests via email (24-hour response)
- Usage analytics tracking
- Feature requests captured
- Bug fixes prioritized

**Week 13**: Pilot Review
- Detailed feedback session with each co-op
- Case study interview and approval
- Conversion to standard subscription or off-board
- Testimonial capture (written + video if possible)

### Success Metrics

**Quantitative**:
- 3-5 pilot partners signed
- 80%+ activation rate (board + members using platform)
- 50%+ weekly active users
- Average 5+ hours/week admin time saved (self-reported)
- 90%+ pilot-to-paid conversion

**Qualitative**:
- "This solved [specific pain point]"
- "We can't imagine going back to [old way]"
- Willing to serve as reference customer
- Willing to do video testimonial
- Referring other co-ops unprompted

### Deliverables

- 3-5 case studies (written)
- 2-3 video testimonials
- Usage data and ROI analysis
- Feature prioritization roadmap based on feedback
- Pricing validation (does $200-250/month feel right?)

## Risks & Mitigation

### Risk 1: Slow Adoption (Co-ops Resist Change)

**Mitigation**:
- Target "change agent" board members (new, younger, tech-savvy)
- Offer free trial period (3 months)
- Provide extensive onboarding and support
- Build strong case studies showing ROI
- Leverage CHF BC endorsement to overcome skepticism

### Risk 2: CHF BC Partnership Falls Through

**Mitigation**:
- Build momentum independently (don't depend on CHF BC)
- Target co-ops directly through other channels
- Partner with regional federations instead
- Position as "trusted by X co-ops" instead of "endorsed by CHF BC"

### Risk 3: Competitor Enters Market

**Mitigation**:
- Move fast: establish brand and customer base before competitor appears
- Deepen moat: domain expertise, CHF BC resources, network effects
- Build switching costs: integrations, historical data, member buy-in
- Maintain product quality and customer satisfaction

### Risk 4: Technical Debt Slows Product Development

**Mitigation**:
- Invest in code quality and documentation early
- Prioritize scalability over rapid feature addition
- Budget for refactoring and technical improvements
- Hire experienced engineers, not just junior devs

### Risk 5: Churn (Co-ops Cancel After Pilot)

**Mitigation**:
- Track leading indicators (usage, engagement, support tickets)
- Proactive outreach if usage drops
- Quarterly business reviews with customers
- Continuous product improvement based on feedback
- Strong onboarding to ensure early wins
